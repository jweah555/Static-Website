About Animals

Owl

Owls are birds of prey known for their distinct appearance, nocturnal habits, and exceptional hunting abilities. They belong to the order Strigiformes, which includes over 200 species.

Last updated 3 mins ago

Bees

Bees are flying insects closely related to wasps and ants, known primarily for their role in pollination and, in the case of the best-known species, the European honeybee, for producing honey and beeswax. They belong to the superfamily Apoidea within the order Hymenoptera.

Last updated 3 mins ago

Porcupine

Porcupines are large, slow-moving rodents best known for their coat of sharp quills used for defense. Modified hairs coated in keratin; can detach easily and are replaced over time. Contrary to myth, porcupines can’t shoot their quills.

Last updated 3 mins ago

Jelly Fish

Jellyfish are gelatinous, free-swimming marine animals that belong to the phylum Cnidaria, class Scyphozoa (true jellyfish), though the term also refers to similar creatures in related classes. They've existed for over 500 million years, making them some of the oldest multi-organ animals on Earth.

Last updated 3 mins ago

HummingBird

Hummingbirds are small, vibrant birds known for their rapid wingbeats, hovering flight, and ability to fly backward—a rare skill among birds. They belong to the family Trochilidae, with over 300 species, primarily found in the Americas.

Last updated 3 mins ago

Squirrels

Squirrels are small to medium-sized rodents known for their bushy tails, sharp claws, and quick movements. They belong to the family Sciuridae, which includes tree squirrels, ground squirrels, chipmunks, marmots, and flying squirrels—more than 270 species in total.
